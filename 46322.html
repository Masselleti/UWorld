Type of injury

Histopathologic changes

Acute neuronal
injury
  • Severe insult (eg, ischemia) leading to cell death
  • Shrinkage of the cell body
  • Pyknosis of the nucleus
  • Loss of Nissl bodies
  • Eosinophilic cytoplasm (red neuron)

Axonal reaction (regeneration)
  • Axon transection
  • Enlargement of the cell body
  • Eccentrically placed nucleus
  • Enlargement of the nucleolus
  • Dispersion of Nissl bodies

Chronic neuronal injury
  • Progressive degenerative disease (eg, ALS, Parkinson disease)
  • Loss of functionally related neurons
  • Reactive gliosis

ALS = amyotrophic lateral sclerosis.
